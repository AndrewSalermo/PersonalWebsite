My Relationship with Social Media and Mental Health

Posted on [4/1/24]

When choosing a topic for this infographic, I aimed to focus on a subject that I could resonate with. In 2023, I realized I would spend an excessive amount of time on apps such as YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram. While I conducted research on different social media platforms, it surprised me to find out that a third of U.S. adults (33%) reported using TikTok. This was a 12 percent point increase from 2021. When checking at my own screen time, I saw I was spending hours on each platform daily, especially on TikTok. While social media is a powerful tool where you can connect with others and learn new things, 90% of my time was spent on DOOM Scrolling, especially before bed. This would cut into my personal time, work life, and even began affecting my sleep. Interestingly, statistics show that the average American checks their phone about 144 times daily, and 75% of us reach for our phones within just five minutes of receiving a notification. I found myself being a part of this statistic and I had become addicted to social media. I decided it'd be best to limit my screen time to an hour each day. After making this change, I became more productive and incorporated better habits into my lifestyle. One of my values in life is to strive to become a better version of myself every day and I hope this infographic inspires someone to better their own social media habits.
